Admissions Funnel Simulator

Reverse-engineer your enrollment pipeline from goal to inquiry

Getting started

1

Set your enrollment goal

Top-right corner of the header — type in how many students you want to enroll. Everything else reverse-engineers from this number.

Example: Type 400 if your class goal is 400 enrolled students.
2

Choose a preset (optional shortcut)

Click any preset to load realistic yield rates for your institution type — great for quick exploration.

Small liberal arts Mid-size university Highly selective Transfer-heavy
3

Drag the yield rate sliders

Each slider controls how many students convert from one stage to the next. Drag left to lower, right to raise — the funnel updates instantly.

Tip: Use your actual historical yield rates for the most accurate pipeline estimate.
4

Read the pipeline results

The right panel shows how many students you need at each stage. The insight box at the bottom identifies your biggest bottleneck stage automatically.

Advanced — what-if mode

5

Switch to "What-if mode"

Click the What-if mode tab at the top. A second set of sliders appears so you can model a scenario change alongside your base rates.

Try this: Lower "Admit → Deposit" by 5% to see what happens if yield drops after May 1st.
6

Read the delta badges

Each funnel stage shows a colored badge comparing what-if vs base. Green = more students needed at that stage. Red = fewer needed (better yield).

Pro tip for admissions teams: Run this before your enrollment committee meeting. Set your actual deposit yield, model a 3–5% drop, and walk in knowing exactly how many additional inquiries your team needs to stay on goal.
